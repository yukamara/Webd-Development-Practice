Yusufu Unisa Kamara

Biography

I joined Clark College in September 2015 as an Economics Instructor in the Business, Economics, Accounting, and Management (BEAM) unit. Before joining Clark College I was an Adjunct Instructor at Baker University in Kansas. I also taught at Avila University in Kansas City, Missouri and Neosho Country Community College in Ottawa, Kansas. I earned my BSc (Honors) in Economics from Fourah Bay College, University of Sierra Leone. I was awarded a Fulbright Scholarship in June 2006 to study for an M.A. in Economics at the University of Kansas. I also earned my Ph.D. from the University of Kansas.

My teaching Interests include Introduction to Economics, Micro- and Macroeconomics, International Economics, and Economic Development.

My primary research interests are in the area of international capital flows and their impact on economic growth and development. I am primarily interested in developing and emerging economies.

I am married and I live with my wife and two kids. I enjoy spending time with my family.
